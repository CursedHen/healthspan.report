# Reflection Note — Healthspan Report

**Student:** Ali Al Hadi Arzouni

Honestly, the past eight weeks on Healthspan Report have been an incredible time — easily the most fun and most formative production engineering experience I've had as a student. I got to ship across an entire editorial publication built on Next.js 16, React 19, TypeScript, Tailwind, Supabase, and a custom RSS ingestion pipeline — from a Figma-aligned redesign of the homepage, articles, videos, and footer, through a full auth-pages refresh (login / signup / forgot-password / reset-password), to a first-class theme system with light, dark, and system modes (and dark as the new default), to an entire authenticated settings surface with account information management and a real password-change flow wired into Supabase auth, and finally a search experience and a thumbnail-fallback hardening pass that made the public site visibly more resilient. The most valuable thing I learned was how to hold a layered, live system (App Router + CSS-variable design tokens + Supabase auth + a three-tier RSS fallback) in my head at once, and how much engineering quality lives in unglamorous places — design tokens, mobile alignment polish, lint cleanup after merges, and a careful contributor handoff doc. I leave the engagement noticeably stronger at design-engineering on a Figma-driven codebase, theming under shared-state constraints, and shipping vertical slices into a repo where multiple contributors are moving at once — and with a clear sense that this is the kind of full-stack, design-adjacent product work I want to keep doing.
